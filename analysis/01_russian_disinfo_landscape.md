# Russian Disinformation Landscape: Overview and Context

## Overview

Following Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine in February 2022, a significant and coordinated disinformation campaign emerged across global social media platforms. Turkey, as a NATO member maintaining active diplomatic relations with both Russia and Ukraine, became a primary target for pro-Russian narratives seeking to influence public opinion and foreign policy.

## Why Turkey?

Turkey represents a uniquely valuable target for Russian information operations:

- **NATO membership** — Undermining Turkish support for NATO weakens the alliance
- **Balanced foreign policy** — Turkey's refusal to join Western sanctions created fertile ground for pro-Russian messaging
- **Bosphorus control** — Russian interest in Turkish Straits policy under the Montreux Convention
- **Large, politically polarized social media user base** — Turkey has over 50 million active social media users
- **Historical narratives** — Existing anti-Western sentiment exploitable by pro-Russian messaging

## Key Disinformation Narratives Targeting Turkey (2022–2024)

### 1. NATO Victimhood Narrative
*"Turkey is being used by NATO against its own interests"*
- Framing Turkey as a pawn of Western powers
- Exaggerating NATO pressure on Turkey over Ukraine policy
- Amplifying domestic anti-NATO voices

### 2. Economic Warfare Narrative
*"Western sanctions will destroy the Turkish economy"*
- Overstating the economic consequences of Turkey's alignment with the West
- Promoting Russia as Turkey's indispensable economic partner
- Targeting Turkish business communities and trade networks

### 3. Historical Revisionism
*"Russia and Turkey share common enemies"*
- Exploiting historical Ottoman-Western tensions
- Framing Russia-Turkey relations as naturally aligned against Western imperialism
- Amplifying pan-Turkic and Eurasianist ideological content

### 4. Military Disinformation
*"Ukrainian military is losing, Western weapons are ineffective"*
- Systematic exaggeration of Russian military successes
- Downplaying or denying Russian atrocities
- Targeting Turkish defense industry professionals and military enthusiasts

## Primary Platforms

- **Twitter/X** — High-volume narrative amplification, bot network activity
- **Telegram** — Coordinated channel networks, harder to moderate
- **YouTube** — Long-form disinformation content, Russian state media
- **Facebook** — Targeting older demographic, community groups

## Key Actors

- Russian state media outlets operating Turkish-language services (RT Turkish, Sputnik Türkiye)
- Pro-Russian Turkish influencer networks
- Coordinated inauthentic behavior networks identified by Twitter/Meta
- Eurasianist political movements amplifying Russian narratives
